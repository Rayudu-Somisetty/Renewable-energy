How Wind Power Works

  • Moving air pushes turbine blades and turns the rotor.
  • The shaft transfers rotation to a generator inside the nacelle.
  • Above cut-in wind speed, electrical output becomes usable.
  • Power moves through wires to the load and lights the bulb.
  • Below threshold wind, the turbine cannot sustain generation.

Generation threshold: 14 km/h

As wind speed meets the requirement , the wind turbine start to rotate and transfered to generator which in turn generates the electricity.
