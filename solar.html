How Solar Power Works

  • Sunlight strikes photovoltaic (PV) cells inside the solar panel.
  • Photons transfer energy to electrons in the semiconductor layers.
  • The panel's internal electric field pushes electrons in one direction, creating direct current (DC).
  • The current travels through the wire path and reaches the load, which lights the bulb in this model.
  • Lower light means lower current flow, so the bulb dims or turns off.
